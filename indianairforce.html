Indian Air Force

The Indian Air Force (IAF) is the air arm of the Indian Armed Forces. It is the world's fourth largest air force in terms of both personnel and aircraft.

History

The Indian Air Force was established on October 8, 1932, as an auxiliary air force of the British Empire and the prefix Royal was added in 1945 in recognition of its services during World War II.

Organization

The Indian Air Force is headed by the Chief of Air Staff (CAS), who is a four-star air officer and reports to the Chief of the Defence Staff (CDS) and the President of India as the Supreme Commander of the Indian Armed Forces.

Aircraft

The Indian Air Force operates a wide range of aircraft, including the Sukhoi Su-30MKI, the HAL Tejas, the Dassault Rafale, the Mikoyan-Gurevich MiG-21, and the HAL Dhruv.
